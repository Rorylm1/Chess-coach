The Randomized Board

A randomizer, not an editor: every game rolls a one-of-a-kind 2D board that's legible by construction. The real generator runs on this page — xmur3+mulberry32 seeded PRNG, six curated OKLCH families, jitter-within-clamps, and a contrast/ΔL backstop. Nothing here is hand-picked; everything you see is generated, then measured.

01 The six families

Variety comes from which family a seed lands in and how much it's perturbed — never from raw colour randomness. Each board below is a real position rolled from a real seed. The chips report the measured guarantees: piece-rim contrast vs both squares ≥ 3:1 and square ΔL ≥ 0.28.

02 Legibility stress test

Forty-eight random rolls. The claim is ugly-by-construction-impossible — so every single one must pass both floors. Each mini-board shows its weakest measured number; any failure turns red.

03 Reroll & naming — three directions

Same generator, three ways to experience the roll. Try each. We port the interaction decision, not the markup.

A · Dealer's roll

one tap · minimal
— —

One verb. The board re-tints with a staggered sweep and a nameplate snaps in. Cheapest possible surface — the board is the UI; the randomizer is a single button under it.

B · The shelf

curatorial · browse & pin
— —

Rolls become a shelf of swatches you browse and pin. More playful and collectible — you pick a board you like rather than accept the first roll. Heavier surface; risks choice-paralysis.

C · Seed field

shareable · the seed is the board
— —

The seed string is the board's identity. Type one, share one — the M7 live-link reuses exactly this, so both players see the same board. Most powerful, least playful at a glance.

Headline finding to port: legibility is guaranteed on the piece rim (a derived near-neutral outline, ≥ 3:1 vs both squares), not the fill — the research doc's "near-white piece ≥ 3:1 vs a bright square" is physically impossible and would ban every classic-wood board. Outlined glyphs (the spec's rim/sheen/emboss treatment) is what frees squares to be bright and varied. Second finding: the mud-zone ban (L .40–.60 ∧ C .05–.12) collides with the family table's own dark squares (e.g. warm-wood dark L.50 C.065); we apply it to the accent/chrome, and govern squares by C ≤ 0.08 ∧ ΔL ≥ 0.28 instead.
